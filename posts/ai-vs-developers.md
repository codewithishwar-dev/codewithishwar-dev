Most developers think AI will replace them.

It won’t.
But it *will* replace developers who stop learning.

Lately, I’ve seen engineers with 10+ years of experience go back to “beginner mode” — not because they had to, but because they chose to.

They’re learning:

* AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.)
* Prompt engineering
* System design at scale

The interesting part?

They didn’t lose their edge.
They combined experience with AI — and became significantly more effective.

Today it’s not:
Experience vs AI

It’s:
Experience + AI = Leverage

---

💡 Practical takeaway:

If you’re a developer, start small:

* Use AI for debugging, not just code generation
* Ask better questions (prompting is a skill)
* Review AI-generated code like a senior engineer
